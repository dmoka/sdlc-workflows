The fourth of Anthropic's five workflow primitives. A central **orchestrator** LLM reads the task and decides — at runtime — what subtasks to spawn and how many workers to delegate them to. Distinct from simple parallelization, where subtasks are decided in advance.

The shape works well in practice for codebase-wide work because the orchestrator doesn't have to commit to a plan before it sees the territory:

```text
You are the orchestrator. Use the Task tool to fan out to up to 10 subagents,
one per concern. After all return, synthesize their reports into a single PR.
```

In Claude Code, the `Task` tool is the orchestrator-workers primitive made concrete. In LangGraph it's a supervisor with `create_supervisor`. In OpenAI Agents SDK it's nested agents with explicit handoffs. The shape is the same; the implementation differs.

**Use for** refactors where the file count isn't known up front, audits across a sprawling repo, "find every place that does X and decide what to do per case." Orchestrator-Workers shines when subtask discovery and execution interleave.

**Avoid for** tasks with a fixed shape known in advance — running tests, formatting code, anything where you can hard-code the worker pool. Use simple parallelization there; the orchestrator overhead isn't worth it.
