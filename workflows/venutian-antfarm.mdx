A multi-agent SDLC framework with governance baked in: 13 agents split across two tiers — seven executives that own policy and six operational specialists that ship code. Released by Robert Dunie under AGPL-3.0; v0.4.2 shipped March 28 2026.

The operational core is a Leadership Triad: a Product Owner agent for prioritization, a Solution Architect agent for technical context, and a Scrum Master agent that paces the flow. Together they drive a nine-step lifecycle for every work item — groom, promote, build, review, fix, deploy, accept, retro, checkpoint — with compliance gates between stages.

The executive tier (CRO, CISO, CEO, CTO, CFO, COO, CKO) sits above operations and writes the rules. Operational agents act within them and can't override.

Agents earn autonomy on a four-rung ladder. **Crawl** proposes every action. **Walk** runs the standard playbook. **Run** has expanded latitude. **Fly** acts freely. A drop in delivery quality pushes an agent back down the ladder.

**Use in** regulated environments where audit trail, policy separation, and progressive trust matter more than throughput.

**Avoid in** prototypes and hackathons — the governance overhead is the whole point and only pays off when compliance is the constraint.
